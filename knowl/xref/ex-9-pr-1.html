Problem 1.

Let \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 4 \\ -1 \amp 2 \\ 1 \amp 4 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\vec b = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\)
🔗
(a)
Show that the system \(A\vec x = \vec b\) is inconsistent.
🔗
🔗
(b)
Determine the vector \(\widehat b = \operatorname{proj}_{\Col(A)}\vec b\text{.}\) Note that the columns of \(A\) are not orthogonal.
🔗
🔗
(c)
Represent \(\widehat b\) as a linear combination of the columns of \(A\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗
(d)
Set up and solve the normal equations for \(A\vec x = \vec b\) to find the least square solution. Compare your result to Problem 1(c).
🔗
🔗
(e)
Fill in the blanks: When you write \(\widehat b = \operatorname{proj}_{\Col(A)}\vec b\) as a linear combination of \(\underline{\hspace{3in}}\text{,}\) then the \(\underline{\hspace{1in}}\) in the linear combination are the entries of \(\underline{\hspace{4in}}\text{.}\)
