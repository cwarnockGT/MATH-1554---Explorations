    Section Exploration 7, due March 15

    Handout Exploration 7, due March 15
    

    Make sure to familiarize yourself with the Exploration Instructions found on the home page.
    🔗
    This exploration contains three separate problems.
    🔗

    Problem 1.

    Find a \(2\times 2\) matrix \(A\) with real entries that has no real eigenvalues. Verify your answer by computing the characteristic polynomial of \(A\) and explaining why it has no real roots.
    🔗
    🔗

    Problem 2.

    For each of the following matrices \(A\) and linear transformations \(T_A(\vec x) = A\vec x\text{,}\) do the following:
    • Find all eigenvalues and a corresponding eigenvector for each eigenvalue without calculation by thinking it out using the linear transformation’s geometric interpretation.
      🔗
    • Graph each eigenvector and its image under the transformation.
      🔗
    • Graph a random non‑eigenvector and its image.
      🔗
    • Verify correctness using matrix multiplication.
      🔗
    • Confirm your intuition by computing:
      • all eigenvalues,
        🔗
      • algebraic and geometric multiplicities,
        🔗
      • a basis for each eigenspace.
        🔗
      You may need to do this step first for any transformation that you cannot visualize.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    (a)
    \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) \(T_A\) is the zero transformation.
    🔗
    🔗
    (b)
    \(A=\begin{bmatrix} 1/2\amp 1/2 \\ 1/2\amp 1/2 \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) \(T_A\) is projection onto \(x_1 = x_2\) line.
    🔗
    🔗
    (c)
    \(A=\begin{bmatrix} \cos(\theta)\amp -\sin(\theta) \\ \sin(\theta)\amp \cos(\theta) \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) \(T_A\) is rotation by \(\theta\text{.}\) Also, state the values of \(\theta\) for which \(A\) has real eigenvectors.
    🔗
    🔗
    (d)
    \(A=\begin{bmatrix} 0\amp 3 \\ 3\amp 0 \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) \(T_A\) is reflect about the line \(x_1 = x_2\) and stretch by a factor of 3.
    🔗
    🔗
    (e)
    \(A=\begin{bmatrix} 1\amp 2 \\ 2\amp 1 \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) hint: try \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}\).
    🔗
    🔗
    (f)
    \(A=\begin{bmatrix} 1\amp 0 \\ 1\amp 1 \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) \(T_A\) is a shear.
    🔗
    🔗
    (g)
    \(A=\begin{bmatrix} 1\amp 0\amp 0 \\ 0\amp 0\amp 0 \\ 0\amp 0\amp 1 \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) \(T_A\) is project to the side-wall.
    🔗
    🔗
    (h)
    \(A=\begin{bmatrix} 0\amp -1\amp 0 \\ 1\amp 0\amp 0 \\ 0\amp 0\amp 1 \end{bmatrix}\text{,}\) \(T_A\) is rotate by \(90^\circ\) about the \(x_3\)-axis.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Problem 3.

    Pick any two transformations from the \(\mathbb{R}^2\) list above (except the matrix in Task 2.a). For each one:
    🔗
    • Apply a single row operation to \(A\) to form a new matrix \(B\text{.}\)
      🔗
    • Compute the eigenvalues of \(B\text{.}\) Ensure that at least one eigenvalue of \(B\) differs from the eigenvalues of \(A\) (this is not guaranteed).
      🔗
    • Compute the corresponding eigenvectors.
      🔗
    • Compare: Are the eigenvectors for \(B\) the same as for \(A\text{?}\) Are they different?
      🔗
    Finish the sentence:
    🔗
    “When you do a single row operation to a matrix \(A\text{,}\) then the eigenvalues … and the eigenvectors … .”
    🔗
